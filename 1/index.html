Project 1

Exhaustive Naive Alignment Algorithm

Firstly, for jpg images which had poor image quality, I performed an exhaustive algorithm, which ran a small square (15x15) across the entire image. I tested both Euclidean distance and NCC as score metrics, but NCC seemed to yield more accurate results; the reason for this is because NCC performs better even when there are changes in contrast or color. Below are the three jpg low qualities, and the exhaustive search seems to work well.

Architectural perspective compressions

For the larger TIF images, the exhaustive search took a very long time to complete, so I performed a pyramid search on these images in order to speed up the process. In this, I shrunk the resolution of the image by a factor of 0.5 multiple times and then performed the same algorithm as above. Additionally, I also removed the edges of the images, scaled by how large they were, so that the algorithm would not get confused by the edges of the images which were not aligned. Below are the tif images. Note: my algorithm worked fine for every image except Emir, which was slightly misaligned; for Emir, I had to increase the amount of pyramid iterations. The time taken was only slightly longer (~25 seconds compared to ~20 seconds for the others), but it resulted in a much clearer image.
